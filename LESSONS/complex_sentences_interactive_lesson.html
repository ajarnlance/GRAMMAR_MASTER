What is a Complex Sentence?

Definition:

A complex sentence is made by joining a dependent clause (incomplete thought) with an independent clause (complete sentence) using a subordinating conjunction.

Dependent Clause: A group of words that has a subject and a verb but does NOT express a complete thought. It cannot stand alone as a sentence.

Independent Clause: A group of words that has a subject and a verb and DOES express a complete thought. It can stand alone as a sentence.

Examples:

Although Ali likes ice cream, he prefers gelato.

I love London although the weather is bad.

Because she studied hard, she passed the exam.

üí° Key Difference: In compound sentences, you join TWO independent clauses (both can stand alone). In complex sentences, you join ONE independent clause with ONE dependent clause (only one part can stand alone).

Subordinating Conjunctions

To create complex sentences, we use subordinating conjunctions. These words show relationships like time, cause, contrast, or condition between the dependent and independent clauses.

Punctuation Rules

Rule 1: Dependent Clause FIRST

When the dependent clause comes at the beginning of the sentence, place a comma after it.

‚úÖ Correct: Even though sales in gelato have increased, profits are marginal.

‚ùå Incorrect: Even though sales in gelato have increased profits are marginal. (missing comma)

Rule 2: Dependent Clause LAST

When the dependent clause comes at the end of the sentence, generally do NOT use a comma.

‚úÖ Correct: Profits are marginal even though sales in gelato have increased.

Exception 1: Use a comma with "whereas" to show contrast.

Some people prefer chocolate, whereas others prefer simpler flavors.

Exception 2: Use commas with non-defining relative clauses (which, who, whom).

I painted my house, which desperately needed some attention.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistake: Remember the position of the dependent clause!

Dependent clause FIRST = COMMA: Because it was raining, we stayed inside.
Dependent clause LAST = NO COMMA: We stayed inside because it was raining.

When to Use Complex Sentences

Use complex sentences to:

  • Show cause and effect relationships (because, since, as)
  • Express conditions (if, unless, provided that)
  • Indicate time relationships (when, after, before, until, while)
  • Show contrast (although, even though, whereas, while)
  • Add variety to your writing and show sophisticated thinking

Compare these:

Simple sentences only:
The weather was bad. I went to the park. I wanted to exercise.

Better (With complex sentence):
Although the weather was bad, I went to the park because I wanted to exercise.

üìù Key Takeaways

  • Complex sentences join one dependent clause with one independent clause
  • Use subordinating conjunctions (although, because, when, if, since, etc.)
  • Comma AFTER dependent clause when it starts the sentence
  • Generally NO comma when dependent clause ends the sentence
  • Exceptions: Use comma with "whereas" for contrast and with non-defining relative clauses
  • Complex sentences show relationships between ideas (cause, contrast, time, condition)
üéØ Practice Tips:
1. Identify which clause can stand alone (independent) and which cannot (dependent)
2. Choose a subordinator that matches the relationship between your ideas
3. Remember: dependent clause first = comma; dependent clause last = no comma (usually)
4. Mix simple, compound, AND complex sentences for the best writing!
